The Executive Pay Gap

Tracking the growing compensation divide between financial leaders and the people they employ.

↑ 1,460% since 1978

In 1965, CEOs earned about 20× more than the average employee. Today, that number exceeds 300×. The expansion of stock-based compensation, weak wage growth, and profit-first culture have driven this divide to historic levels.

While shareholders and boards justify high compensation as performance-based, data suggests the multiplier effect has outpaced both inflation and productivity gains — especially for frontline workers.

“From 1978 to 2021, CEO compensation grew by 1,460% — compared to just 18% for the average U.S. worker.”

Source: Economic Policy Institute, 2023

Contributing Factors

  • Equity-based compensation: Options and stock awards dominate modern CEO pay packages.
  • Low wage bargaining: Worker pay has grown slowly even as productivity increased.
  • Benchmarking: Executive pay is set competitively — leading to escalating averages industry-wide.

Why It Matters

A widening executive pay gap fuels inequality, erodes trust, and can damage long-term company performance. Policymakers and investors are increasingly demanding transparency and accountability from corporate boards.

Additional Sources: Harvard Business Review, OECD, World Inequality Report
