The Executive Pay Gap

Tracking the growing divide between corporate leaders and the employees who power their companies.

↑ 1,460% CEO pay growth since 1978

The executive pay gap refers to the widening earnings disparity between top financial executives — particularly CEOs — and the average employee. In the U.S., this gap has grown dramatically over the past four decades.

“From 1978 to 2021, CEO compensation grew by 1,460%, while typical worker pay grew just 18%.”
— Economic Policy Institute, 2023

Why Does the Gap Exist?

  • Stock-based compensation: Most CEO pay comes in the form of equity.
  • Weak wage growth: Worker wages have not kept pace with inflation or productivity.
  • Peer benchmarking: Compensation is often set by comparison, pushing averages upward.

Policy Options

  • Mandated pay ratio disclosures (already required by the SEC)
  • Progressive taxation on excessive executive bonuses
  • Expanded worker representation on boards
  • Incentives for equitable compensation practices
