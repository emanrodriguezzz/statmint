The Executive Pay Gap

In recent decades, executive pay has grown dramatically. In 1980, the average CEO earned roughly 40 times more than the average worker. By 2023, that multiple had climbed above 300× in many U.S. companies — driven by stock options, incentive packages, and growing market concentration.

Meanwhile, real wages for most workers have remained relatively stagnant. This growing pay gap raises concerns around economic fairness, corporate accountability, and long-term sustainability — and it's a trend echoed across many developed economies.
