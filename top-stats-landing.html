Wealth Gap

CEO Pay vs. Worker Wages

The gap between CEO compensation and average worker pay has widened significantly over the past four decades. In 1980, the CEO-to-worker pay ratio was about 42:1. As of 2023, it has surged to over 340:1 in many large U.S. companies.

This increasing inequality highlights how executive pay continues to grow far faster than the wages of most employees, raising questions about fairness, economic sustainability, and corporate accountability.

Source: Economic Policy Institute, 2023
